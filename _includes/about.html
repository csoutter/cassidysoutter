About

Some of the highlights of my career and my transition into computer science.

  • 1998

    Back to the Beginning

    I was born in 1998 to a father working at Microsoft and a mother working at a paper processing company. I have one older sister and had two dogs at the time. We lived outside of Charlotte in North Carolina and later moved to Apex, North Carolina

  • 2007

    Looking Forward

    Growing up I wanted to be many things. I wanted to be a teacher, a veternarian, and happy. Something that was important to me, even at a young age, was helping people and trying to give back to others.

  • May 2016

    Finishing High School

    In high school, I wanted to study music and be a classical trained opera singer. Music was an integral part of my life at the time and still is. I sang in choirs all over the state, attended Governor's School for Choral Music, went to different universities to study with the voice faculty there, and studied music in my spare time. However, I was interested in programming. I attended a small high school that didn't offer many STEMP opportunities, so I taught myself to use HTML/CSS and Python, trying my best to understand something that felt like impossible magic.

  • August 2018

    Starting at UNC-CH

    In the Fall of 2016, I started college at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I wanted to study both music and computer science and minor in Spanish. Like most first year students, I soon decided in 2017 to pursue only computer science, with minors in Information Systems and Spanish for Business. Computer Science began to become an integral part of my life and the more I did it, the more I grew and fell in love with it. Technology is limitless. Being a part of the field of technology makes me feel like I speak the language of magicians, and I love it.

  • January 2018

    Girls Who Code

    At the beginning of 2018, my involvement with computer science began to shift. I went from a devoted student with 3 jobs in customer service, to a computer science student devoted to learning more both inside and outside of the classroom. I had been searching for opportunities to use my knowledge and passion outside of the classroom, eagerly looking for projects and organizaions to be a part of. I soon found Girls Who Code in 2017 and began teaching in 2018. Girls Who Code is a nonprofit organization that empowers young middle and high school girls to learn the fundamentals of programming. We offer these girls a supportive place to learn, make mistakes and grow, helping to close the gender gap in tech one girl at a time.

  • March 2018

    CS+Social Good

    Soon after starting with Girls Who Code, I began work as a founding member of CS+Social Good, a student led organization started at UNC in the spring of 2018 that empowers nonprofit to achieve their goals of social good by giving them knowledge and technology. As a founding member, I played a key role in developing both our first and current websites, working on a team to ensure cohesiveness and functionality. Our club is continuing to grow and expand as we all try to use technology for good.

  • April 2018

    Rewriting the Code

    Lastly, I found Rewriting the Code, a nonprofit organization that helps support college women pursuing tech to have access to resources and opportunities. The graduation rate of women in tech is significantly lower than that of men and Rewriting the Code (RTC) is working to change that. With more than 1000 women across the world, we are all looking to empower each other and create a difference for the women coming after us. I'm currently a 2018-2019 Rewriting the Code Fellow, through which I've found many leadership opportunities with RTC and have grown as a leader and mentor through working with these amazing women.

  • Now!

    What I'm up to now!

    In the summer of 2018, I started an internship with SAS as an R&D technical intern working on the Visual Analytics software. Through this opportunity, I was offered the position of a year round intern so that I could continue my work as an intern. It has been an amazing opportunity and I have grown so much through working with seasoned developers and experts in the field. I am now SAS certified too! My language proficiency includes Java, C, Javascript, TypeScript, HTML/CSS, SAS, MIPS Assembly Language, and Python. I'm a junior at UNC-CH, pursuing a BS in Computer Science with minors in Information Systems and Spanish for Business. My expected graudation date is May 2020. What else should you know about me? I love jazz, knitting, dogs, cooking, The Office, and volunteering. I'm a tinker, insatiably curious, a leader, a passionate go-getter, and a creator. I'm excited to see where my life takes me next.
